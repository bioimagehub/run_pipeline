Images acquisition

I-SPT uses the localization microscopy methodology to isolate the signal of single molecules.

Localization microscopy as opposed to bulk imaging uses the photo-physics of the fluorophore to sequentially illuminate single molecules and detect them.

  • Bulk imaging: Fluorescent molecules (green) are numerous and cannot be detected individually:

    Figure 1429. 

  • Localization microscopy: only a few molecules are emitting in the imaging channel (red) and its position can be individually accurately estimated:

    Figure 1430. 

Different methods (specific buffer, photo activating UV laser, ...) can be used so that only a small sub-sample of fluorescent molecules are ‚Äúon‚Äù. In the nucleus, the signal can be significantly enhanced by tilted illumination:

Figure 1431. 

Any localization microscopy hardware is eligible to I-SPT module. The following protocol is dedicated to acquisition with photoactivatable proteins.

Photoactivatable proteins can be switched to a new fluorescent state upon activation with UV laser. Single molecule regime with low activation power:

Figure 1432. 

Here, we show how the illumination sequence of NIS elements can be used to accurately illuminate a sample tagged with photoactivatable proteins and reach the single molecule regime required by I-SPT. We use two lasers:

  • Activation laser needs to illuminate the sample with very low power between frames so that a fluorescent does not ‚Äúpop out‚Äù during acquisition.

  • Illumination laser needs to illuminate the sample with very high power during frame acquisition for high signal to noise ratio.

Exposure time needs to be set prior to illumination sequence settings for the camera. Typical exposure time for target moving inside the nucleus is 10 ms.

Illumination sequence

Figure 1433. 

Launch Acquire > Illumination Sequence.

  1. Assign current camera settings (ROI, frame rate).

  2. Create an image channel and drag and drop it into the phase sequence.

  3. Drag and drop excitation laser.

  4. Drag and drop activation laser. Right click Edit >Custom. Activation shall start at ‚Äúexposure end‚Äù and stop at ‚Äúnext exposure start‚Äù.

    Set a low level of activation at first (5%). This level will be adjusted if necessary during acquisition by double clicking on the activation cell:

    Figure 1434. 

    Figure 1435. 

  5. Set a number of loops.

  6. Check the sequence with the oscilloscope view:

    Figure 1436. 

  7. Run the sequence.
